Mobile Music Application

This project was a final project for my COGS 187A: Usability and Information Architecture class. Here is the website. The goal of this project was to interview users who used different music applications on their mobile phones and to build an app that allowed users to make and find playlists of their choice.

My role in this project was to interview certain people who used music applications on their phones, such as Pandora and Soundcloud. I interviewed them to see what they used these apps for and how they would use them to do certain tasks.


A snapshot of an interview and a user scenario created by the user.

After interviewing people, we would find out steps they took to do certain things like making a playlist, adding songs to a playlist, and deleting a playlist. I recorded these findings and found patterns that these users found to be most common among the users.


A snapshot of different apps used to do the same tasks. In this case, creating a playlist for a romantic date.

My group and I then built an app that took all these factors into account. Another goal was to make the process of doing tasks like creating and deleting a playlist simpler than the apps that we looked into. We ran into some difficulties figuring out how to use the Invision software because none of the group members had ever used it before. It took a while to figure out but once we messed around with it for a while, we knew what we were doing and were about to continue with the project.



Our first scenario was to create a playlist and add a song to the newly created playlist. Our path of execution was to click the hamburger at the top left (three dashes). We go to Playlists and then click the "+" button, type in the name of the playlist, and click "OK" to complete the action. Then to add a song, we go to Genres, click Classical and choose an artist. In this case, we click Beethoven and click the three dots to open a list of options. We click "Add to Sleep" which is more direct than the apps that we looked into. This functionality takes the most recently created playlist and gives a quick link for the user to add to this playlist. If the user does not want to add to this playlist, the user can click "Add to Playlist" which gives a list of more playlists that the user has. After adding the song to the playlist, it returns to the list of Beethoven songs for further action.



The other scenario we had to create in our prototype was to delete an existing playlist. We go to the list of playlists and click the three dots to open a list of options for the playlist. In this case, we click the three dots of "Homework" which opens a list of options. The options are "Rename Playlist" and "Delete Playlist". We click "Delete Playlist" which then brings the user back to the list of playlists. In our project, we do not worry about the functionality. That is, we do not worry about actually making the Homework playlist disappear, but we worry more about how the user does what the user intends to do and how easy it is to get to that point.

In this scenario, my group and I discussed about how in iOS applications, to remove/delete something, many times the user has to swipe right or left to see the delete button. However, for non-iPhone users, this is not intuitive. Therefore, to combat this, we created the three dots throughout the app which we believed were more intuitive for users who wanted to delete a playlist to click it to open more options. This is something that some apps that we looked into did not have, and we believe this is a better approach for helping the user as much as possible.

Another difficulty we ran into was trying to make our prototype "better" than the apps that we looked into. There were things that we did that made the user click one less button, but there were times when we realized that there was no way to make a task more simple. These apps are the most popular and most commonly used, and if there was a problem with the way it did something, I'm sure they would have fixed this problem already to the best of their abilities.

The final product was a mobile application that allowed users to add a playlist, add songs to this playlist, and remove an existing playlist. This was a great learning experience for me to learn about peoples' habits when they use their phones for music and exactly how they go about utilizing them to their liking. This also helped me gain experience working in groups and interacting with each other to get the best ideas out and put them to action.
